# Session 3: RUN (Execution & Experience)

**Focus:** The "During" and "After" phases of the event.
**Learning Objective:** Facilitate live event dynamics, manage crises, design fair assessments, and ensure long-term impact.

-$-$-

# Part 1: The Architecture of Time

Time must be engineered as a finite resource to prevent "cognitive drift" and burnout in students.

-$-$-

# The Dual Timeline Strategy

* **Public Agenda:** Visible milestones for students to provide psychological reassurance.
* **Run of Show (RoS):** A minute-by-minute internal script for staff tracking invisible logistics like food arrival.

-$-$-

# Managing the Energy Curve


-$-$-

# Combating the "Trough"

The mid-event slump requires specific interventions to maintain morale:
* **Mentorship Rounds:** Deploy mentors to uncover silent struggles or technical blockers.
* **Fun Interstitials:** Schedule fun mini-events to combat sugar crashes and frustration.

-$-$-

# 12-Hour vs. 24-Hour Models

* **12-Hour Sprint:** Lowers liability and staffing costs; ideal for first-time organizers.
* **24-Hour Overnight:** Offers deep immersion but requires shift work, sleeping areas, and strict supervision.

-$-$-

# Part 2: Engineering Social Dynamics

Team formation should not be left to chance but engineered to ensure cross-functional skills.

-$-$-

# Team Matching: Skills Scavenger Hunt

* Assign roles (Coder, Designer, Project Manager) using colored lanyards.
* **Rule of 3:** Mandate that every team must have at least three different roles represented.

-$-$-

# Pedagogical Icebreakers

* **"Bad Ideas Only":** Brainstorming the worst possible apps to remove the fear of judgment.
* **"Marshmallow Challenge":** A rapid build exercise to reveal team dynamics and identify dysfunctional groups early.

-$-$-

# Part 3: Day-of Facilitation

The teacher shifts from "Instructor" to "Event Architect," fostering self-regulated learning.

-$-$-

# The "Guide on the Side"

* **Socratic Troubleshooting:** Ask "What have you tried?" rather than fixing code for students.
* **Scope Management:** Aggressively help teams cut scope to focus on an achievable Minimum Viable Product (MVP).

-$-$-

# Mentorship Protocols

* **The Hands-Off Rule:** Mentors must never touch a student's keyboard; they guide via diagrams or pseudocode.
* **Proactive Rounds:** Mentors walk the floor every hour to ask "What is your biggest blocker?"

-$-$-

# Part 4: The Finale (Judging & Rubrics)

Transform the coding spree into a communication challenge where students synthesize their work.

-$-$-

# Judging Logistics: The Hybrid Model

1.  **Science Fair (Expo):** Judges circulate to tables for 3-minute demos, ensuring every student gets face time.
2.  **Stage Pitch:** Only top finalists present to the entire room to keep the audience engaged.

-$-$-

# Designing the Rubric

Balance learning with execution to encourage ambition:
* **Innovation & Difficulty (50%):** Reward novel ideas and technical challenges, even if buggy.
* **Polish & Impact (50%):** Assess UX design, real-world utility, and the "Wow" factor.

-$-$-

# The 3-Minute Pitch

* **The Hook:** Start with the problem, not the technology.
* **The Demo:** A mandatory live demonstration of the software is required (no slides-only pitches).

-$-$-

# Part 5: Crisis Management


-$-$-

# Technical Failure Protocols

* **WiFi Failure:** Deploy "Sneakernet" (USB drives) and extend deadlines; frame it as a "Retro Hacker Challenge."
* **Platform Crash:** Switch to Google Forms or paper submissions to avoid ruining the sense of achievement.

-$-$-

# Interpersonal & Safety Protocols

* **Team Meltdown:** Use the SOAR method (Stop, Observe, Assess, React) and separate students if necessary.
* **Two-Adult Rule:** Never allow a student to be alone with a single adult in a private space.

-$-$-

# Part 6: Post-Event Legacy

The value of the hackathon is generated in the weeks following the event through reflection and integration.

-$-$-

# Sustainability & Recognition

* **GitHub Portfolio:** Teach students to "clean" their code and publish it as a credential for college.
* **Curriculum Integration:** Use hackathon prototypes as the basis for AP Computer Science tasks or senior capstones.

-$-$-

# Part 7: Final Simulation

**"The Perfect Storm"**
Participants will simulate a 2:00 PM crisis scenario involving a WiFi crash, a team argument, and a sleeping student.
